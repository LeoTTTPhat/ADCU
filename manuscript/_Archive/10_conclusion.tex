\section{Conclusion}

This paper frames foundation-model unlearning as counterfactual behavioral indistinguishability for trustworthy AI pipelines. \method{} combines clean-reference testing, provenance graphs, graph-adaptive probe allocation, causal component interventions, black-box behavioral tests, and confidence-bounded deletion-risk reporting. The repeated RealRAG, FineTuneSim, HybridReal, LoRA-SFT, DenseRAG, TOFU-style, natural-data, and attack-suite results show that narrow exact-match, canary-only, retriever-only, membership-style, and extraction-only audits miss important residual-dependence channels. The resulting framework provides a concrete path toward reproducible deletion verification for RAG, fine-tuned, and hybrid foundation-model systems.
